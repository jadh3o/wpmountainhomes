Java Haus

A Woodland Park staple on Midland Avenue serving craft espresso, breakfast burritos, and house-baked pastries every morning.

About Java Haus

Java Haus has become one of Woodland Park's most beloved morning destinations. Located right on West Midland Avenue in the heart of downtown, it draws a loyal crowd of locals who come for the well-pulled espresso, hearty breakfast burritos, and a pastry case that changes with the seasons. Whether you are grabbing a quick latte on the way to Pikes Peak or settling in with a book, Java Haus delivers a warm, unpretentious mountain-town coffee experience.

The shop's interior blends rustic wood accents with bright natural light, creating a space that feels both cozy and energizing. It is a popular spot for morning meetups, remote work sessions, and the kind of slow Saturday mornings that make living in Woodland Park so appealing.

What Makes It Special

Java Haus stands out for the quality and consistency of its espresso program. The baristas know their craft, and the menu includes a solid selection of lattes, cappuccinos, and seasonal specials. But the food deserves attention too: the breakfast burritos are a local favorite, and the fresh-baked pastries and bagels round out a menu that goes well beyond a typical coffee counter.

The location is also hard to beat. Midland Avenue is the main commercial artery of Woodland Park, and Java Haus sits right in the middle of it. Walk out the door and you are steps from local shops, restaurants, and trailheads. For homeowners in nearby neighborhoods like Woodland Heights or Sunnywood Manor, it is an easy morning walk.

Address & Directions

Java Haus is located at 116 W Midland Ave, Woodland Park, CO 80863. It sits on the main downtown strip with street parking available along Midland Avenue. Coming from Highway 24, turn south onto Baldwin Street and you will find it within a block.

Living Near Java Haus

Homes within walking distance of Java Haus place you at the center of Woodland Park's daily life. The surrounding neighborhoods, including Woodland Heights, Sunnywood Manor, and the Midland Area, offer a mix of mountain cottages, updated family homes, and properties with views of Pikes Peak. Being close to downtown means quick access to groceries, restaurants, the library, and community events throughout the year.

Average home prices in these walkable neighborhoods range from the mid-$300s to the mid-$600s, making it an attainable option for buyers who want convenience and mountain living in the same package.
