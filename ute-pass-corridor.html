The Ute Pass Corridor

The scenic Highway 24 corridor connecting Woodland Park to Colorado Springs — through Cascade, Chipita Park, and Green Mountain Falls.

About the Ute Pass Corridor

The Ute Pass corridor along Highway 24 connects Woodland Park to Colorado Springs, passing through some of the most charming and scenic mountain communities in the Pikes Peak region. This stretch includes Cascade, Chipita Park, Green Mountain Falls, and Crystola — each with its own distinct character and real estate opportunities.

Green Mountain Falls

A charming, artsy mountain village between Woodland Park and Manitou Springs. Green Mountain Falls features a community gazebo lake, creative residents, cozy cabins, and a tight-knit creative community. It's one of the most affordable mountain communities in the region, attracting artists, writers, and anyone seeking a quieter pace of life.

Cascade & Chipita Park

Nestled along Fountain Creek on the Ute Pass corridor, these communities offer mountain retreat living that's halfway between Woodland Park and Colorado Springs. Properties range from historic cabins and cottages to updated mountain homes. The area has a more rustic, forested feel compared to central Woodland Park.

The Commute Factor

One of the biggest advantages of the Ute Pass corridor is the commuting sweet spot. You're closer to Colorado Springs than Woodland Park proper — typically 15–20 minutes to the west side of the Springs — while still living in a true mountain setting. This makes corridor communities popular with professionals who work in Colorado Springs but want mountain living.

Considerations

The Ute Pass corridor can experience heavier traffic during summer tourism season and winter storms. Some properties have steeper driveways and more limited access. Flood zones along Fountain Creek should be evaluated. Despite these factors, the corridor offers some of the best value in mountain real estate near Colorado Springs.
